Experience

Since November 2025, I have been a Senior Applied Scientist at The Trade Desk, conducting research on large-scale identity graphs with billions of nodes and edges.

From June 2021 to October 2025, I was a Senior Applied Scientist at Microsoft. I fine-tuned LLaMA-2 with LoRA for automatic fraud-rule generation, engineered pipelines that convert natural-language features into dense embeddings using pretrained LLMs, and developed attention-based and graph neural network systems for transactional data, entity linkage, and anomaly discovery. My work also included bot-detection models based on device fingerprinting and mouse behavior, plus Azure pipelines for customer fraud scenarios.

In early 2021, I worked as an Applied Scientist at Amazon, building production-grade machine learning models on AWS to prevent denied parties from transacting on Amazon.

Earlier, I worked as a Data Scientist at Microsoft, where I built proof-of-concept fraud-protection pipelines with Databricks, Azure Pipeline, Cosmos DB, Scope, and Power BI. I also worked at Halliburton Landmark as a Software Development Engineer and intern, building web/cloud UI systems, API services, optimization workflows, and computer-vision prototypes for wellbore schematics.
